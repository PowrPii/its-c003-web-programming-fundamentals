E-commerce

Online purchasing and selling of products and services over an electronic network, primarily the internet. This is known as electronic commerce, or simply “e-commerce”. It involves online transactions between businesses to consumers (B2C), as well as transactions between businesses (B2B). With its impact on how people conduct business, buy goods, and engage with each other in the marketplace, e-commerce has grown to be a major part of the global economy. The term of e-commerce is also used in reference to the transactional processes that make up online retail shopping.

In the last two decades, widespread use of e-commerce platforms such as Amazon and eBay have contributed to substantial growth in onlune retail. In 2011, e-commerce accounted for 5% of total retail sales, according to the U.S Census Bureau. By 2020, with the start of the COVID-19 pandemic, it had risen to over 16% of retail sales.

Application of AI in E-commerce

In the world of e-commerce, artificial intelligence (AI) has become a disruptive force that is changing how companies run and how consumers interact with them online. The integration of AI technologies into e-commerce platforms has brought forth a new era of efficiency, personalization, and innovation. Artificial Intelligence (AI) is revolutionizing the e-commerce industry with its tailored product recommendations, intelligent chatbots, and predictive analytics. The connection between modern machine learning algorithms and online shopping not only improves the user experience but also streamlines operational operations, resulting in a more adaptable and competitive marketplace.

- Virtual Voice Assistants And Chatbots

AI-driven chatbots are widely used by e-commerce platforms to offer real-time customer care. For instance, companies that assist entrepreneurs in better managing their online stores by handling duties like marketing, order tracking, and customer communication (ChatGPT). An “immediate” response within 10 minutes is rated as necessary or very important by 90% of customers.

Customer support is always available even during weekends and holidays with AI-powered chatbots. While human support team is away, a chatbot can answer simple questions or perform simple tasks, route customers to suitable knowledge base materials, or allow them to leave messages for agents and schedule callbacks.

When customers go to a shop, a shopkeeper or sales associate helps them find what they want. This makes the shopping experience quick and easy for customers. It has been difficult to have this convenience in online shopping, but not anymore. Now, AI chatbots and voice assistants are so advanced that they can understand customers' queries and be as helpful as human sales associates. Moreover, they can service your customers 24 hours a day, 7 days a week and in multiple time zones and languages. AI technology is bridging the gap between physical and online shopping and boosting the scalability of ecommerce businesses worldwide. Since customers are already familiar with apps like Google Assistant, Apple's Siri and Amazon's Alexa, they are comfortable interacting with artificial intelligence in ecommerce, too.

Chatbots and virtual assistant can provide prompt responses 24/7, allowing your live support agents to address more complex customer service issues. Besides, chatbots and virtual assistant can collect customer data, such as sizing and the reasons for inquiry, which can help inform product development and improve customer service. Chatbots and virtual assistants may manage basic transactions, process orders, and send clients tailored offers, which makes handling a high number of requests easier across various channels from a physical store, online or through a mobile app.

- Personalized Product Recommendations

Artificial intelligence systems examine user behavior, past purchases, and preferences to offer tailored product recommendations. AI is used by businesses like Amazon to make product recommendations to clients based on their browsing and purchasing habits.

With personalized product recommendations, shop owners can personalize customer interactions and provide more relevant online shopping experiences that will drive higher conversion rates, average order values, and customer loyalty.

Personalized product recommendations use data from past customer behavior, browsing history, and purchase history to suggest products. For example, Natural language processing (NLP) based AI can understand online shoppers’ language and images to match them with desired products. AI-powered features like “People also purchased” or “Customers also viewed” can suggest complementary products based on size, colour, shape, fabric, and brand.

Customization is no longer an extra or unique feature. Customers demand it now, no matter when or where they shop. Salesforce reports that 66% of consumers think businesses should be aware of their requirements and expectations. Customizing a purchasing experience by hand for each individual customer is not feasible. But today, artificial intelligence (AI) in e-commerce can gather personal data from clients by examining their behavior on e-commerce platforms and the information they disclose to the website. The same artificial intelligence technology may ascertain a specific customer's likes and preferences and suggest things to them that they are more likely to buy using a mathematical computer-generated algorithm based on several historical data points. This has the potential to greatly improve sales and customer engagement on the e-commerce platform.

- Dynamic Pricing

AI algorithms are employed by some e-commerce business to implement dynamic pricing strategies by using AI to analyze market conditions, competitor pricing, and other factors to adjust product prices in real time for optimal competitiveness and profitability.

With dynamic pricing, you may modify your offers and prices in response to changes in global supply and demand, competitor activity, and user behavior in real time. By using AI's capabilities, you can predict when discounts will be most effective and dynamically calculate the lowest discount needed to close a deal.

For example, increasing your prices when your competitors' stocks are low. When consumers want a products right away, but it's unavailable on one website, they will more than likely be willing to pay a higher price if they know they can get it from your store sooner.

Example, amazon is one store that fully utilizes a dynamic pricing strategy. Whenever its competitors offer promotions or discounts, they regularly adjust their products prices by up to 20%. However, it does so gradually to ensure maximum profitability based on sales forecasts. This allows it to remain the cheapest and maintain control over its profit margins.

Dynamic price optimization is the process of offering products at various prices that adjust according to market conditions. Pricing on products and services are dynamically changed based on competitive pricing, supply and demand, sales requirements, and conversion rates.

Currently, by leveraging machine learning algorithms, pricing can be efficiently automated to find and maximize price points by using sophisticated formulas, leveraging all of the available proprietary and public data of a market.

Benefits of Using AI in E-commerce

- Increase Sales

AI can help you create a more efficient sales process by gathering and analysing customer data to personalize your sales funnel. With more data, you can engage with the right prospects with the right message at the right time.

- Reallocation of Time and Resources

Your email messages, order fulfilment, customer support, and payment processing may all be automated with the use of AI. You may spend more time inventing and less time on maintenance by implementing automations to lower costs and increase operational efficiency. Supply chain managers can minimize errors by up to 50%, lost sales, and product unavailability by up to 65% by using AI-powered forecasting.

- Cost Savings and Operational Efficiency

AI lowers costs and boosts operational effectiveness by automating and optimizing a variety of activities. This is especially helpful for jobs like logistics, inventory control, and customer service.

Challenges of Using AI in E-commerce

- Data Privacy

Artificial intelligence (AI) algorithms use customer data to generate tailored suggestions and forecasts. Data security and privacy are raised by the collection of this information. AI also puts businesses at risk for unexpected security breaches and disclosures involving private intellectual property.

Massive volumes of user data must be gathered and analysed in order to deploy AI. This creates questions regarding the security and privacy of data, particularly in light of the increasingly strict legislation like GDPR.

- High Initial Investment

AI implementation can be expensive. It requires financial outlays for maintenance, expertise, and infrastructure. Furthermore, there's a chance that AI solutions won't always provide a profitable return on investment.

- High Implementation Cost

Integrating AI systems into an e-commerce platform can require significant upfront investment. Small and medium-sized businesses may find the initial costs prohibitive.
